# Panic Parking Lot

> When anxiety comes: **write the thought → label it → convert it to one action.** That's the whole protocol. The brain is biased toward failure-evidence; this page is the counterweight.

**How to use:** add a row the moment a spiral starts. Labels: `regret` · `fear` · `fantasy` · `comparison` · `real task`. Then do (or schedule) the one action and close the tab in your head.

## The worked examples (keep these — they're the pattern)

| Panic thought | Type | Action |
|---------------|------|--------|
| I've wasted so much time | regret | Do one 45-min block now. No drama, just repayment. |
| What if the launch flops | fear | List 3 things I can actually control this week; do the first. |
| I need it all figured out *now* | fantasy | Add it to the one-year vision, not today. |
| Everyone my age is ahead of me | comparison | Compare to last-year me, not to a highlight reel. |

## The reframes (read when spiraling)

**Why one event feels like everything.** The mind attaches too many symbolic questions to a single moment — *Will I make it? Did I waste years? Will people see me as a success?* That's too much load for one interview, launch, or decision. **One gate, not the kingdom.**

**Stop making every event a referendum on your worth.** A launch is not "am I good enough?" — it's only "can I ship this one thing well this week?"

**Convert regret into repayment.** Regret says "I wasted time." Repayment says "Fine — today I'll do 90 minutes." No drama. Just repayment.
