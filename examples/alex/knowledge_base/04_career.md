# 04 — Career

> What Alex does, where they're headed, and the moves that matter.

## Current role

Product engineer at a mid-size software company in Portland. Full-stack (TypeScript / React / Node / Postgres), but the real strength is product sense — owning a feature from rough idea to shipped, and making the details feel considered. Comfortable and respected, but honestly coasting a little.

## The arc

Started in a junior full-stack role → grew into the person who quietly owns the tricky user-facing features → now at the edge where the next step is either more scope at work or making something of their own. **Fieldnotes is the bet on the second path** — without quitting the first.

## The current career mission

**Operation Launch Week:** ship Fieldnotes to 50 beta users by 2026-08-22. The deeper goal is to prove — mostly to themselves — that they can own something end-to-end and finish it, instead of endlessly refining. That story is also the one to bring to the next 1:1 at work.

## Comp / targets

Income ~$6.5k/mo. Not chasing a big jump this year; the priority is *ownership and finishing*, not comp. Any Fieldnotes revenue is upside.

## Strengths to lead with

- Product taste — knows when something feels wrong and can say why.
- Ships considered work; low-drama; good to pair with.
- End-to-end range: design instincts through to a working backend.

## Gaps I'm closing

- **Finishing.** The pattern is 90%-and-polish-forever. Fieldnotes is the deliberate practice ground for actually shipping.
- **Visibility.** Does good work quietly; needs to narrate it more — starting with the launch.
