# 05 — Relationships

> The people who matter, and how Alex shows up for them.

## The close circle

- **Sam — partner.** Live together in Portland. The relationship is good and steady, but it's the first thing that quietly suffers when Alex disappears into a deadline. Sam doesn't need grand gestures — just an Alex who's actually *there* in the evening, not mentally debugging.
- **Parents.** A standing Sunday call. Warm, uncomplicated, grounding. Alex protects it even during crunch.
- **A couple of close friends** — the kind you can go quiet with for weeks and pick right back up. Low-maintenance, high-trust.

## How I want to show up

Present, not just around. Under a deadline the instinct is to bring the laptop to the couch; the better move is to close it and be fully there for an evening. Consistency over intensity — a reliable weekly rhythm beats occasional big efforts.

## Standing commitments

- **Friday dinner with Sam** — unhurried, no laptop. Protected through launch.
- **Sunday call with parents.**
- Reach out to the two close friends at least once a month, even just a voice note.

## Where I tend to slip

When Fieldnotes gets stressful, Alex goes internal — physically present, mentally gone. It never actually helps the work, and it always costs something at home. The tell: three evenings in a row on the laptop after dinner. That's the cue to stop.
