# One-Year Vision

> Where I want to be twelve months out. Concrete enough to check against.

**Horizon:** July 2026 → July 2027

## The one-line version

Fieldnotes has real, paying users and a small steady rhythm; I'm still at my day job but on better terms; pottery is a genuine weekend joy (and a few sales); Sam and I are more settled; and money is quieter than it is today.

## By area

| Area | Where I am now | Where I want to be in a year | The first move |
|------|----------------|------------------------------|----------------|
| Career | Product engineer, comfortable but coasting | Recognized for owning something end-to-end; clearer growth path | Ship Fieldnotes launch; use it in my next 1:1 |
| Fieldnotes | Beta, ~0 users, launching Aug | 100+ active users, small paid tier, sustainable weekly cadence | Get to 50 beta users by 2026-08-22 |
| Health | Walk + read mostly holding; workouts patchy | 3 workouts/week as a reliable habit, not a good-week thing | Book two fixed gym slots I don't negotiate |
| Relationship | Good, but I get absorbed under deadlines | More present; a standing unhurried evening each week | Protect Friday dinners with Sam |
| Pottery | A few pieces sold, casual | A small consistent output I'm proud of; maybe a local market stall | Keep the Saturday slot sacred through launch |
| Finance | ~$6.5k/mo, small brokerage, emergency fund, student loan | Emergency fund at 6 months; student loan noticeably down | Automate an extra loan payment |

## Money bands

- **Emergency fund:** minimum 3 months → target 6 months of expenses.
- **Student loan:** chip it down steadily; no heroics, just automation.
- **Fieldnotes:** any revenue is upside this year; success is *users*, not dollars, in year one.

## What has to be true for this to happen?

- I actually launch Fieldnotes instead of polishing it forever.
- I keep the base (sleep, walk) even during the crunch.
- I run one thing hot at a time — Fieldnotes now, not Fieldnotes *and* a pottery business *and* a new framework.

## What would quietly derail it?

Trying to do everything at once (my classic). A launch that slips indefinitely because it's never "ready." Letting the deadline eat the relationship and the walks — which always backfires within two weeks.
