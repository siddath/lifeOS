# North Star

> The compass, not the plan. Revisit quarterly.

## What is my actual North Star?

A calm, craft-centered life in Portland with Sam: work I'm proud of (building products that feel considered, not rushed), a small side-project that's genuinely mine, weekends at the pottery wheel, close relationships I actually show up for, and enough financial steadiness that money stays quiet. Built at a pace I can hold — not by sprinting until I burn out and start over.

## What should never be compromised?

1. **The relationship with Sam** — presence, not just proximity. Not making them live with a permanently stressed version of me.
2. **Health basics** — sleep, the morning walk, moving my body. The floor everything stands on.
3. **Honesty** — with myself about what I actually shipped vs. planned, and with the people I work with.
4. **Craft over speed** — I'd rather make one good thing than six rushed ones.

## What gets paused while I'm chasing the current mission?

Pottery sales beyond the Saturday slot · any new side-idea · the "should I learn Rust" rabbit hole · reorganizing my note system for the fourth time.

## What gets accelerated?

Fieldnotes to 50 beta users · shipping something visible every week · keeping the walk-and-read routine that keeps me sane under a deadline.

## What is simply noise?

Refreshing the analytics before there are any users · comparing Fieldnotes to funded startups · re-reading the same launch-strategy blog posts · redesigning the landing page instead of shipping the invite flow · deciding, un-deciding, and re-deciding the pricing model.

## The operating principle

> **Think once → Build → Measure → Improve.** Stop re-planning. Ship evidence weekly.

## And finally — enjoy the ride

This launch is not the whole point. The point is the life around it — the morning walk, the smell of the studio, Sunday calls home, making something with my hands. Intensity without enjoyment is just anxiety with a to-do list. Hold the plan loosely enough to actually live it.
